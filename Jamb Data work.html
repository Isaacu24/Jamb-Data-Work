T-test to determine if there is a significant difference between scores for male and female

-Null hypothesis: There is no significant difference in the average Jamb scores of male and female students

-Alternative hypothesis: There is significant difference in the average Jamb scores of male and female students

In[29]:
male_students=data[data['Gender']=='Male']['JAMB_Score']
female_students=data[data['Gender']=='Female']['JAMB_Score']
In[33]:
t_stat, p_value=stats.ttest_ind(male_students, female_students)
t_stat, p_value
Out[33]:
(-0.8620553522496285, 0.3887073890384244)

Interpretation

  • A t-test value of -0.86 indicates that the difference between the means of the two groups (gender, and JAMB scores) is relatively small compared to the variability within those groups. The negative value suggests that the mean of the first group is slightly lower than that of the second group, but the difference is minimal.

  • The p-value of 0.38 is much higher than the common significance level of 0.05. This means we fail to reject the null hypothesis (H0), which states that there is no significant difference in the average Jamb scores of male and female students

  • Practical Interpretation: The observed difference (indicated by the t-test of -0.86) is likely due to random variability rather than a true effect of gender. In practical terms, this suggests that gender does not have a meaningful impact on JAMB scores in this data set.

  • Practical Interpretation: Despite the low t-test result, the very small p-value indicates strong evidence against the null hypothesis(rejecting the null hypothesis). It suggests that the difference in attendance rate between the school types is unlikely to be due to random chance.
